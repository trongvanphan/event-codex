# Vietnam Context: Startup Ecosystem

## The 2024–2025 Structural Reset
The Vietnamese startup ecosystem has shifted from rapid, less-selective expansion toward **capital efficiency and sustainability**.
*   **Funding Trends:** The market has experienced a correction phase. Total venture capital investment in 2024 reached approximately $494 million, contracting further to roughly $215 million in 2025. This reflects increased investor caution and a focus on proven business models with real cash flows.
*   **Concentrated Capital:** Capital has become more concentrated, with a strong preference for later-stage rounds. The top 10 deals in 2025 accounted for approximately 72% of the total invested value.

## Resilient Sectors and Growth Hubs
Despite tightening capital, several sectors continue to thrive:
*   **EdTech & ClimateTech:** Education technology, renewable energy, and electric vehicles remain highly attractive to investors.
*   **Retail & E-commerce:** Continued growth supported by high digital adoption.
*   **AI Startups:** Emerged as a significant area of focus, attracting notable early-stage investment.

## Ecosystem Maturation
*   **Global Standing:** Vietnam’s startup ecosystem reached its highest-ever global position at 50th in the Global Startup Ecosystem Index (GSEI) in 2026, cementing its status as a fast-growing hub.
*   **Hub Development:** Growth is spreading beyond Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi to cities like Da Nang. Ho Chi Minh City entered the top 100 global startup cities.
*   **Infrastructure & Policy:** The government continues to foster a supportive environment through Resolution No. 57. The launch of local corporate venture funds, such as Vingroup's **VinVentures** in late 2024, is sustaining the ecosystem.
