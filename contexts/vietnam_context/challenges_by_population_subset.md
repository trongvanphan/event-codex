# Vietnam Context: Challenges by Population Subset

Understanding the specific pain points of different demographics is crucial for empathetic product design in the Vietnamese market.

## 1. Gen Z (Born 1997–2012)
*   **The Skills-Expectation Gap:** Gen Z demands flexibility, purpose-driven work, and rapid career progression. However, they face a highly competitive job market where their academic training often doesn't match practical industry requirements (the skills gap), leading to high turnover and frustration.
*   **Financial Anxiety:** Despite being digitally savvy, many face economic pressure from inflation, housing costs in major cities, and the pressure of the "hustle culture" promoted on social media, leading to rising stress and mental health concerns.
*   **Information Overload:** Constantly connected, they suffer from digital fatigue and the pressure to maintain curated online personas.

## 2. Millennials (Born 1981–1996)
*   **The "Sandwich Generation" Pressure:** Millennials are the primary stabilizing force in the economy but often face the dual burden of caring for aging parents (due to shifting family structures and limited eldercare infrastructure) while raising young children.
*   **Work-Life Integration:** As mid-level managers or established professionals, they struggle with work-life balance in a corporate culture that often normalizes long hours and constant digital availability via chat apps like Zalo.
*   **Upskilling Pressure:** They face the constant need to upskill to remain relevant against the influx of cheaper, digitally native Gen Z talent and the rapid adoption of AI.

## 3. The Elderly (60+ Years)
*   **Digital Exclusion:** As services (banking, healthcare, government services) rapidly digitize, the elderly population faces severe digital exclusion due to low digital literacy and UI/UX designs that do not account for accessibility (small fonts, complex navigation).
*   **Healthcare Access:** As the population ages, accessing quality, affordable healthcare and managing chronic conditions in an overburdened medical system is a primary challenge.
