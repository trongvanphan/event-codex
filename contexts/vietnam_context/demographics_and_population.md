# Vietnam Context: Demographics and Population

## Overview of the Population (2024–2025)
Vietnam’s demographic landscape is characterized by a massive, stabilizing population that is highly engaged with digital technology.
*   **Total Population:** Reached approximately **102.3 million** by the end of 2025, maintaining its position as the third most populous country in Southeast Asia and the 16th globally.
*   **The "Golden" Population Structure:** Vietnam is currently in a highly advantageous demographic phase where the working-age population (15–59 years) accounts for approximately 62.7% of the total. However, experts warn that the country is expected to exit this phase by 2036 as the population ages.
*   **Fertility and Aging:** The total fertility rate has declined to **1.93 births per woman**, below the replacement level of 2.1. Major urban centers like Ho Chi Minh City report even lower rates. Consequently, the share of elderly residents (60+ years) is steadily increasing.

## Key Demographics for Digital Products

### Gen Z (Born 1997–2012): The Digital Natives
As the first true digital natives, Gen Z in Vietnam has grown up with smartphones and social media as an intrinsic part of their identity.
*   **Tech Identity:** They are the trendsetters, driving adoption of new visual-first platforms (e.g., TikTok, Threads).
*   **Primary Motivations:** They demand *authenticity* and gravitate toward brands that align with their personal values (e.g., sustainability, social impact). They trust peer recommendations over traditional advertising.

### Millennials (Born 1981–1996): The Digital Adaptors
Millennials witnessed the transition from traditional to digital media and are often the primary household decision-makers.
*   **Tech Identity:** The stabilizing force in the workforce and consumer market. They adopt platforms primarily for convenience and connection.
*   **Primary Motivations:** Value stability, career growth, convenience, and seamless online experiences that save them time.
*   **Digital Behavior:** Rely heavily on personalized services (like ride-hailing apps and e-wallets) to streamline busy lives.
