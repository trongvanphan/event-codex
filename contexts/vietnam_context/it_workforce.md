# Vietnam Context: IT Workforce and Skills Gap

## The Core Challenges in 2024-2025
Despite the massive boom in the IT sector, Vietnam faces a significant workforce challenge characterized by a persistent skills gap and a mismatch between the supply of graduates and complex industry requirements.

### 1. Persistent Shortage
During the 2024–2026 period, the market faced an estimated shortage of **150,000 to 200,000** skilled IT professionals. While approximately 50,000 to 80,000 IT students graduate each year, only about **30% are considered immediately employable** for complex enterprise roles without extensive retraining.

### 2. The Skills Mismatch
*   **Theory vs. Practice:** A primary concern is that many academic programs remain overly theory-based. Graduates lack essential practical experience, project-based thinking, and the ability to adapt to fast-paced corporate environments.
*   **Soft Skills:** Employers frequently cite a lack of critical thinking, teamwork, and foreign language proficiency (especially English and Japanese) as major barriers to hiring.

### 3. The Specialization Gap
While there is a steady supply of junior-level talent for basic web development and QA, there is a severe scarcity of high-quality, experienced professionals in emerging fields:
*   Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning
*   Cloud Computing architecture
*   Cybersecurity
*   Data Science

### 4. Intensifying Competition
Competition for senior-level talent is fierce. The rise in global demand for high-skilled Vietnamese talent—particularly remote work opportunities from markets like the U.S., Singapore, and the U.K.—has further tightened the local labor supply and driven up wages for top-tier engineers.

## Driving Factors and Outlook
The rapid boom in e-commerce, digital banking, and AI adoption has outpaced educational reform. To bridge this gap, industry leaders are focusing on "Action-Based Learning," university-enterprise partnerships, and massive upskilling/reskilling initiatives.
