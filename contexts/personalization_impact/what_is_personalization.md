# Personalization Impact: What is Personalization?

## Defining Personalization
In the context of digital products, **personalization is the real-time tailoring of digital experiences—content, interfaces, messaging, and product features—to individual users.** This is powered by Artificial Intelligence (AI) and machine learning algorithms that analyze a user's historical behavior, preferences, and real-time context.

It represents a shift from a "one-size-fits-all" broadcast model to a 1-to-1 conversational model where the product adapts to the user.

## Customization vs. Personalization
It is critical to distinguish between customization and personalization:
*   **Customization is User-Driven:** The user is in control and explicitly makes choices to alter the experience. *Example:* A user goes into settings and selects "Dark Mode" or manually filters a search for "Shoes under $50."
*   **Personalization is System-Driven:** The system is in control and implicit. The AI predicts what the user wants based on data. *Example:* Netflix automatically surfacing action movies on a user's homepage because they watched three action movies last week, without the user ever clicking a "show me action movies" button.

## The Goal of Personalization
The ultimate goal of personalization is to reduce **Time-to-Value (TTV)** and **Cognitive Load**. By anticipating what the user wants to do next, the product removes friction, making the user journey feel effortless and intuitive.
