# Advanced Prompts for Workshop Learners

To ensure learners have sufficient depth when creating their product artifacts (Personas, User Journeys, PRDs), consider providing these additional contexts and workshop prompts to challenge their thinking:

## A. Data Privacy & The "Creepy Factor"
*   **Context:** While users demand personalization, there is a fine line. Approximately **64% of consumers** have experienced personalization that felt invasive or "creepy" (such as ads based on private conversations or excessive third-party tracking). The shift is moving toward "Zero-Party Data" (data intentionally shared by the user).
*   **Workshop Prompt:** *How might we design AI personalization features that are transparent, give the user control over their data preferences, and build trust rather than exploiting privacy? What is the "value exchange" we are offering the user in return for their data?*

## B. The "Cold Start" Problem
*   **Context:** AI machine learning models need historical data to personalize effectively. However, brand new users have no data history on the platform.
*   **Workshop Prompt:** *How do we design an onboarding experience that quickly gathers enough explicit (asked) and implicit (observed) data to kickstart the recommendation engine without causing friction or making the onboarding process too long?*

## C. Data Silos & Integration Infrastructure
*   **Context:** Effective personalization requires unified, clean, first-party data. In many existing enterprises, marketing data, product usage data, and financial data live in completely different systems (silos) that don't talk to each other.
*   **Workshop Prompt:** *When designing your technical architecture or PRD, how will your product ingest, unify, and process data from these various sources in real-time to power the AI model effectively? (Think about Customer Data Platforms - CDPs).*

## D. The Filter Bubble Effect
*   **Context:** Highly optimized recommendation algorithms can trap users in an "echo chamber" or filter bubble, only showing them content they already agree with or products they've already bought, limiting discovery of new items.
*   **Workshop Prompt:** *How can you design your recommendation algorithm to balance exploitation (showing them what they are guaranteed to like based on past data) with exploration (introducing them to novel, serendipitous content or products)?*
