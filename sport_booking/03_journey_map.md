# Journey Map

## Player Journey

### Discover

**Trigger:** A friend proposes a game, the user wants exercise, or an open period appears in the user's schedule.

**Channels:** Friend referral, Zalo booking link, social content, venue QR code, search, or app store.

**Need:** Quickly know whether useful inventory and partners exist.

**Product response:** Allow guest browsing by current area and time. Show actual availability and representative final prices immediately.

### Aware

**Evaluation:** The user compares the app with calling a familiar venue or posting in a group.

**Objections:** Are listings accurate? Is the price final? Will refunds work? Is the venue good? Why does the app need personal data?

**Product response:** Show live availability timestamp, verified venue information, amenities, cancellation terms, ratings, and concise explanations of personalization.

### Convert

**Steps:** Select sport -> choose area/time -> compare results -> select court or open game -> sign in -> pay/deposit -> receive confirmation.

**Emotional state:** Interested but impatient.

**Product response:** Preserve guest selections through signup, support local payment methods, show total and per-person cost, and minimize form fields.

### Retain

**Return triggers:** One-tap rebook, weekly group habit, relevant waitlist alert, open-game invitation, loyalty progress, or off-peak value.

**Churn risks:** Inaccurate inventory, spam, poor match quality, hidden fees, slow refunds, or a failed first booking.

**Product response:** Learn preferred times and venues, offer adjacent alternatives, let users tune notifications, and make issue resolution visible.

### Advocate

**Sharing behavior:** Invite teammates, share an open game, post a result, or refer another organizer.

**Motivation:** Easier coordination, group credits, status as a reliable organizer, or a free/off-peak reward.

**Product response:** Create a shareable Zalo link, referral credit after a completed booking, and group profiles for recurring play.

## Venue Owner Journey

### Discover

**Trigger:** Visible off-peak emptiness, cancellation losses, or excessive manual booking work.

**Channel:** Direct sales, venue association, referral from another owner, or player demand.

**Product response:** Lead with recovered revenue and operational simplicity, not "AI."

### Aware

**Evaluation:** The owner compares commission, setup work, payment timing, and risk of channel conflict.

**Product response:** Demonstrate a calendar, price controls, payout schedule, and an incremental-revenue pilot.

### Convert

**Steps:** Verify venue -> define courts and hours -> publish inventory -> set price floors and cancellation policy -> accept first booking.

**Product response:** Provide assisted onboarding and allow a limited inventory pilot before full migration.

### Retain

**Return triggers:** Fewer empty slots, easier operations, and measurable incremental margin.

**Churn risks:** Double-booking, low-quality customers, commission exceeding value, or discounts cannibalizing direct bookings.

**Product response:** Inventory synchronization, deposits, customer reliability signals, holdout measurement, and editable automation.

### Advocate

**Motivation:** Revenue proof, reduced staff workload, and network demand.

**Product response:** Venue referral credits, case-study reporting, and multi-location controls.
