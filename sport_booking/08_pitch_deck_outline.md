# Pitch Deck Outline

## Presenting

### Hook

Every empty court-hour expires at the end of the hour. At the same time, players nearby are still messaging groups, calling venues, and failing to organize a game.

### Credibility

Position the team around local marketplace operations, mobile product execution, venue relationships, payments, and data-driven optimization. Do not lead with AI credentials alone.

## Problem Statement

### For players

- Availability and pricing are fragmented across calls and chat.
- Organizers carry the coordination and payment burden.
- A court is not useful when the group is incomplete.
- Generic discounts ignore actual schedule, distance, and quality preferences.

### For owners

- Court inventory is perishable.
- In the supplied stadium model, other-time occupancy is only 40%, leaving 1,920 of 3,200 off-peak court-hours unused over four weeks.
- Weekend demand after 16:00 is already modeled at 100%, so indiscriminate discounting would give away peak revenue.
- Owners lack demand visibility and fear cannibalizing full-price bookings.
- Manual scheduling makes accurate marketplace inventory difficult.

### Core insight

The missing product is a demand orchestration layer, not simply a coupon app.

## Solutions

### Player experience

- Browse live nearby inventory immediately
- See the best match and best-value alternatives
- Understand exactly why a recommendation appears
- Book, pay, invite, and split with the group
- Join waitlists and later open games

### Owner experience

- Publish inventory and policies
- Protect peak demand with floors and caps
- Recover cancellations automatically
- See occupancy, realized price, and incremental contribution

### Product magic

"Your usual 18:00 court is full. Play at 17:00 eight minutes away, save 40,000 VND, and invite your group in one tap."

## Desirability

### Context evidence

- Vietnam has a large, digitally engaged working-age population.
- Gen Z responds to affordability, social proof, and visual mobile experiences.
- Millennials value convenience and seamless services that save time.
- Users increasingly expect context-aware recommendations but also transparency and control.

### Evidence to collect before claiming product-market fit

- Interviews and diary studies with players
- Venue occupancy exports or manual logs
- Landing-page demand by neighborhood
- Concierge booking pilot
- Alternative-time and open-game experiments
- Owner willingness-to-pay and commission tests

## Viability

### Business model

- Commission on completed bookings
- Owner software and analytics subscription
- Corporate wellness and multi-venue programs later

### Growth loop

More venues create better player choice -> more players create better occupancy -> better demand data improves recommendations -> better economics attract more venues.

### Go-to-market

- Start with a dense neighborhood, not an entire country.
- Onboard 5-10 venues with meaningful off-peak supply.
- Recruit existing organizers and venue communities.
- Use group invitations as the primary acquisition loop.
- Expand only when inventory accuracy, repeat booking, and local liquidity meet thresholds.

### Economic proof

The four-week synthetic baseline for 10 courts is:

- 4,480 available court-hours
- 2,320 booked court-hours
- 51.79% total occupancy
- 278.4M VND revenue
- 139.2M VND contribution before fixed and platform costs

A 5% discount lowers contribution per booking from 60,000 to 54,000 VND. If applied to all off-peak bookings, occupancy must increase from 40% to 44.44% just to preserve contribution. This supports targeted, measurable offers rather than blanket coupons.

Show incremental occupied court-hours, revenue per available court-hour, contribution after incentives, and venue retention. Gross bookings alone are insufficient.

## Feasibility

### Initial system

- Standard marketplace inventory and payment stack
- Rules-based ranking using explicit preferences and real-time availability
- Owner campaign controls
- Event analytics and holdout experiments

### AI path

- Predict demand and cancellation risk after enough data exists.
- Rank options using observed behavior and context.
- Use bounded contextual experimentation to select offers.
- Use conversational AI later for search and owner explanations.

### Trust and compliance

- Same published price for the same court-hour and eligibility
- User preference, consent, explanation, and deletion controls
- Server-side owner guardrails
- No claim of automated pricing until performance and fairness are validated

## Closing

The goal is not to make every court cheap. It is to make the right court-hour discoverable to the player who can use it, at a price that creates new demand and leaves both player and owner better off.
