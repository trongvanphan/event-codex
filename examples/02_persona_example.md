# Persona: "Anxious Alex"

## Descriptive
*   **Narrative:** Alex is a 16-year-old high school junior. They are currently taking AP Calculus and doing well, but their real passion is digital art and graphic design. They constantly hear on the news about AI taking over jobs and are terrified of picking a major that will be obsolete or won't pay off their future student loans.
*   **Demographic:** 16 years old, suburban high school student, middle-class family.
*   **Psychographic:** Pragmatic but creative. Values financial stability but doesn't want to be bored. Highly digitally native (spends 4 hours a day on TikTok/Discord).

## Motivations
*   Wants to find a career that blends their strong math skills with their creative interests (e.g., UX Design, Architecture, Creative Coding).
*   Needs data to "prove" to their parents that their chosen major is a viable career path.

## Interactions
*   Logs into the school's Learning Management System (LMS) every single day to check homework and grades.
*   Ignores mass emails from the school counseling office because they feel generic.

## Pain Points
*   Information overload: Searching "what to major in" yields millions of conflicting results.
*   Lack of personalization: Standard career aptitude tests feel outdated (e.g., "You should be a forester or an accountant").
*   High anxiety about the "ROI" of college.

## Moments that Matter
*   Course selection week for Senior year.
*   The first conversation with parents about college tuition budgets.
*   Receiving standardized test scores.
