# Design Thinking Document: LMS Career Pathway Builder

## Empathize
**Users:** High school students (Sophomores & Juniors).
**Context & Feelings:** They are highly stressed, overwhelmed by the sheer volume of college choices, and anxious about their future. They feel pressure from parents to pick a "lucrative" major, but want to follow their passions. They feel they aren't getting enough 1-on-1 time with overloaded school guidance counselors.
**Challenges:** They don't know how their current classes translate to real-world jobs.

## Define
**Core Problem:** High school students lack personalized, data-driven, and continuous guidance to connect their unique academic strengths, personal interests, and future labor market realities to make an informed decision about their college major.

## Ideate
*   *Idea 1:* A chatbot counselor.
*   *Idea 2:* A virtual career fair in VR.
*   *Idea 3:* An AI-driven dashboard integrated directly into their daily Learning Management System (LMS) that continuously maps their grades, extracurriculars, and self-reported interests to career trends. **(Selected Idea)**

## Prototype
**"The Pathway Explorer Dashboard"**
We will build a frontend prototype of a new tab within the existing LMS. The prototype will demonstrate the user flow: taking a rapid interest assessment, connecting it to mock "historical grades," and generating a personalized card UI showing the Top 3 recommended majors/careers with a breakdown of *why* (skills match) and *what's next* (market opportunity).

## Test
We will run a usability test with 15 high school juniors. 
**Feedback sought:** 
1. Do they find the "Why this fits you" explanation accurate and trustworthy?
2. Does seeing the "Market Opportunity" data increase or decrease their anxiety?
3. Is the UI intuitive enough to use without a teacher's instruction?
