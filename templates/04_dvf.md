# DVF Framework

*Purpose: The DVF (Desirability, Viability, Feasibility) framework acts as a reality check for any product idea. It ensures that a product is not only loved by users (Desirable) but also makes financial sense for the company (Viable) and can actually be built by the engineering team (Feasible). An idea must hit the intersection of all three to succeed.*

## Desirable (for users)
*   **What it does:** Evaluates whether the target audience actually wants or needs the solution.
*   **Why it's important:** You can build a brilliant, highly profitable piece of software, but if no one wants to use it, it will fail.
*   **Details to include:** Evidence from user research, survey data, market trends, or prototype testing. What specific user pain point does this alleviate? Is the pain acute enough that they will change their current behavior to use your product?
*   **Impact:** Ensures the product achieves "Product-Market Fit."

## Viable (for businesses)
*   **What it does:** Evaluates the business case. Does this product align with the company's strategic goals and generate a return on investment (ROI)?
*   **Why it's important:** A product that users love but bankrupts the company is not a good product. It must be sustainable.
*   **Details to include:** The revenue model (e.g., subscription, ads, freemium). How it impacts the business (e.g., reduces customer support costs, increases market share, generates direct revenue). What are the anticipated customer acquisition costs (CAC) vs lifetime value (LTV)?
*   **Impact:** Secures stakeholder buy-in and funding. It proves the product is worth investing in.

## Feasible (for engineers)
*   **What it does:** Evaluates the technical reality. Can we actually build this with the time, talent, and technology available?
*   **Why it's important:** Prevents product managers from promising science-fiction features that engineers cannot deliver.
*   **Details to include:** Required technical infrastructure, data availability (do we have the data needed for the AI?), regulatory constraints (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA compliance), and estimated engineering effort (time/headcount). Are there third-party APIs we rely on?
*   **Impact:** Sets realistic timelines and informs the technical architecture. Prevents "scope creep."
