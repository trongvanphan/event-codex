# Journey Map

*Purpose: A Journey Map visualizes the end-to-end experience a user has with a product, from the moment they realize they have a problem to long-term advocacy. It breaks down silos, forcing the team to see the product not as isolated features, but as a continuous narrative.*

## Discover
*   **What it does:** Maps the very first stage where the user realizes they have a need and begins looking for a solution.
*   **Why it's important:** Helps identify where to intercept the user. If they can't find you, the rest of the product doesn't matter.
*   **Details to include:** The user's internal trigger (e.g., feeling stressed), external triggers (e.g., seeing an ad), and the channels they use to search (Google, App Store, Word of Mouth).
*   **Impact:** Informs top-of-funnel marketing and SEO strategies.

## Aware
*   **What it does:** The stage where the user evaluates your specific product against competitors.
*   **Why it's important:** This is where the value proposition must shine. Users are highly skeptical here.
*   **Details to include:** What are they reading? (Landing pages, reviews). What are their objections? (e.g., "Is this too expensive? Is it secure?").
*   **Impact:** Dictates the content of landing pages, app store screenshots, and trust-building UI elements.

## Convert
*   **What it does:** The critical moment the user decides to sign up, download, or purchase.
*   **Why it's important:** Any friction here causes massive drop-off. This must be the smoothest part of the journey.
*   **Details to include:** The steps required to convert (e.g., filling out a form, linking a credit card). Note the exact emotional state (excited but cautious) and any technical hurdles.
*   **Impact:** Drives the design of the onboarding flow, checkout process, and "Time-to-Value" optimization.

## Retain (Retent)
*   **What it does:** The ongoing, day-to-day usage of the product after the initial conversion.
*   **Why it's important:** Acquiring a user is useless if they churn a week later. Retention is the true measure of product-market fit.
*   **Details to include:** What brings them back? (Push notifications, habit loops, core utility). What causes them to churn? (Bugs, lack of fresh content).
*   **Impact:** Informs the core feature set, CRM strategies (email lifecycles), and gamification elements.

## Advocate
*   **What it does:** The final stage where a loyal user becomes a promoter of the product to others.
*   **Why it's important:** Organic, word-of-mouth growth is the cheapest and most effective form of acquisition.
*   **Details to include:** How do they share it? (Referral links, social media, telling friends). What motivates them to share? (Financial reward, social status, genuine love for the product).
*   **Impact:** Drives the creation of referral programs, social sharing features, and community building.
