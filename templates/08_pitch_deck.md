# Pitch Deck Outline

*Purpose: The Pitch Deck is a storytelling tool. Whether pitching to venture capitalists for funding or to internal executives for budget approval, it condenses all product research, strategy, and design into a compelling, persuasive narrative. It must convince the audience that the problem is real, the solution is brilliant, and the business case is sound.*

## Presenting
*   **What it does:** Establishes the context and the speaker's credibility.
*   **Why it's important:** The audience needs to know why *you* are the right person to build this.
*   **Details to include:** Presenter's role. More importantly, establish a **Hook**—a shocking statistic, a relatable anecdote, or a bold statement that grabs attention in the first 10 seconds.
*   **Impact:** Sets the tone for the entire presentation and captures audience attention immediately.

## Problem Statement
*   **What it does:** Clearly and vividly describes the pain point the product solves.
*   **Why it's important:** If the investors/executives don't believe the problem is painful or widespread, they won't care about the solution.
*   **Details to include:** Make it emotional and data-driven. (e.g., "Users are wasting 10 hours a week on X, costing the industry $5B annually").
*   **Impact:** Creates the "villain" of the story that your product will defeat.

## Solutions
*   **What it does:** Introduces your product as the answer to the problem.
*   **Why it's important:** This is the big reveal. It must be easy to understand within seconds.
*   **Details to include:** The core value proposition. High-quality screenshots or a GIF of the prototype in action. Avoid technical jargon; focus on the magic of the user experience.
*   **Impact:** Generates excitement and visualizes the future state.

## Desirability
*   **What it does:** Proves that users actually want this.
*   **Why it's important:** Mitigates market risk. Investors want proof that people will use it.
*   **Details to include:** Pull from the *Persona* and *DVF* templates. Show quotes from user research, survey results, or feedback from prototype testing.
*   **Impact:** Validates the product-market fit.

## Viability
*   **What it does:** Proves the business case.
*   **Why it's important:** Mitigates financial risk. How does this make money or save money?
*   **Details to include:** Pull from the *DVF* and *Roadmap* templates. Discuss the revenue model, target market size, competitive advantage, and potential ROI.
*   **Impact:** Convinces executives that funding this product is a smart financial investment.

## Feasibility
*   **What it does:** Proves that your team can actually build this technically.
*   **Why it's important:** Mitigates execution risk. A great idea is worthless if it can't be built.
*   **Details to include:** Pull from the *PRD* and *DVF*. Briefly explain the technical architecture (e.g., "We leverage existing OpenAI APIs and our proprietary database"). Highlight the team's capabilities.
*   **Impact:** Builds trust in the team's ability to execute and deliver on time.
