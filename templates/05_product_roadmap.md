# Product Roadmap

*Purpose: The Product Roadmap is a strategic communication tool. It translates the high-level product vision into an actionable, sequenced plan. It aligns stakeholders (executives, marketing, sales) and the development team on what is being built, why it's being built, and when roughly to expect it.*

## Vision
*   **What it does:** Sets the ultimate, long-term destination for the product.
*   **Why it's important:** Provides inspiration and a "North Star." When daily decisions get complicated, the team can look at the vision to realign.
*   **Details to include:** A single, powerful sentence describing the future state of the world once your product is fully realized. (e.g., "To organize the world's information and make it universally accessible.")
*   **Impact:** Drives team motivation and long-term strategic alignment.

## Strategy
*   **What it does:** Explains *how* you will achieve the vision. What is your unique angle or competitive advantage?
*   **Why it's important:** Prevents a random assortment of features. Strategy dictates what you will *not* build just as much as what you will build.
*   **Details to include:** The high-level approach (e.g., "We will win by prioritizing mobile-first personalization over desktop enterprise features").
*   **Impact:** Acts as a filter for feature requests. If a feature doesn't fit the strategy, it gets cut.

## Capabilities (Themes)
*   **What it does:** Groups individual features into high-level "Themes" or "Epics" that deliver specific value.
*   **Why it's important:** Roadmaps shouldn't be laundry lists of 100 tiny features. Grouping by capability keeps the focus on user value rather than Jira tickets.
*   **Details to include:** Broad buckets of work (e.g., "Enhanced AI Recommendations," "Frictionless Checkout," "Admin Analytics Dashboard").
*   **Impact:** Makes the roadmap readable and understandable for non-technical stakeholders.

## Success Metrics
*   **What it does:** Defines exactly how you will measure if the capabilities you shipped actually worked.
*   **Why it's important:** Prevents "shipping to ship." It ensures the team is outcome-driven rather than output-driven.
*   **Details to include:** Specific KPIs using frameworks like HEART (Happiness, Engagement, Adoption, Retention, Task Success). E.g., "Increase Weekly Active Users by 15%."
*   **Impact:** Holds the team accountable to actual business and user outcomes.

## Time Horizon
*   **What it does:** Provides a rough chronological sequence for the capabilities.
*   **Why it's important:** Manages expectations across the company. Sales and marketing need to know when to promote features.
*   **Details to include:** Avoid exact dates (e.g., "October 14th"). Use broader horizons like "Now / Next / Later" (agile) or "Q1 / Q2 / Q3" (enterprise).
*   **Impact:** Creates a sense of urgency for the "Now" column while keeping the "Later" column flexible as market conditions change.
